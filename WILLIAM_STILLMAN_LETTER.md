# William Stillman Letter

This page records the family letter that appears to have informed Sima about the death of the surviving aunt in Los Angeles.

## Why this letter matters

- It identifies a sender: `William Stillman`
- It gives a Los Angeles address
- It is postmarked `22 FEB 1980`
- It points directly to the memorial location:
  - `Mount Sinai Memorial Park`
  - `5950 Forest Lawn Dr.`
  - `Los Angeles, CA 90068`
  - `Wall-Crypt 61165`

This letter is one of the strongest surviving U.S. branch clues in the case.

## Envelope details

- **Sender:** `William Stillman`
- **Address:** `432 South Curson Avenue, Los Angeles, California 90036, U.S.A.`
- **Postmark:** `22 FEB 1980`
- **Recipient:** `Dear Sima Berman`
- **Additional addressee text:** `Estate of ...` (not fully legible)

## Page 1

**Date:** `2-21-80`

**Translation:**

> To Dear Sima,
>
> We received your letter and were very happy to hear from you after so long.
> We miss you and we think about you a lot.
>
> Not everything is easy here with us. We have been through a difficult time, and there are many changes in the family.
> We are trying to move on despite the difficulties.
>
> We remember everything the family has been through, and you will always remain a part of us.
>
> We hope you are feeling well and that your life in Israel is going well.
> We would very much like to maintain more regular contact.

## Page 2

**Translation:**

> We want to tell you about what has been happening here lately.
>
> [Probably a relative]'s condition has been getting worse lately.
>
> We were with her as much as we could, but unfortunately we were unable to help more.
>
> It was very difficult for all of us.
> The whole family came together, and it was a very painful moment.
>
> We also remember her connection to you,
> and we know how important she was to you.
>
> Her memory will always remain with us.

## Page 3

**Translation:**

> We want you to know exactly where she is buried,
> so that you can know and remember:
>
> Mount Sinai Memorial Park
> 5950 Forest Lawn Dr.
> Los Angeles, CA 90068
> Wall-Crypt 61165
>
> This is where she is commemorated.
>
> If you ever want to come or find out more details — this is the place.
> We will make sure that her memory is preserved.

## Page 4

**Translation:**

> We hope you will be strong and enjoy your life.
>
> Despite the distance, you are always part of the family.
>
> The connection between us is very important to us.
>
> We would be very happy if you would write to us again,
> and tell us how you feel and how life is for you.
>
> Send greetings to everyone.
>
> With love,
>
> [Signature not fully legible — probably a family member from the American branch]

## Research note

At this stage, the exact identity of `William Stillman` is still unresolved. He may have been:

- a relative
- connected to the surviving aunt's household
- involved in the funeral or memorial arrangements
- or part of the American family branch that remained in contact with Sima

Identifying him may help identify the aunt and reconstruct the surviving U.S. branch of the family.
