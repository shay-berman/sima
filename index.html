Genealogy Research Repository

Sima Berman Birth Family Investigation

This site is the web version of the repository tracking the research into Sima Berman's birth family, surname transition, Kovel origins, and archive outreach.

Sima Berman is my grandmother. My family and I are researching our family roots to understand what happened to her father and siblings during the Holocaust, and to identify whether other relatives may have survived and left additional family branches.

A key open lead is Sima's only known surviving aunt, who lived in Los Angeles and remained in contact with Sima until around 1980. We are trying to identify her, learn her story, and find anyone who knew her or is connected to her through the family network in the United States. We are also searching for William Stillman, who sent a letter to Sima about the aunt and may hold important clues about this surviving branch.

Main Goal

Identify the birth family of:

  • Sima Berman
  • later official surname: Krakowski
  • possible earlier surname:
    • Buxenbaum
    • Buxenboim
    • Nussbaum

Current research focus:

  • reconstruct the original family from Kovel / Kowel / Volhynia
  • resolve the surname contradiction between Krakowski and Buxenbaum/Nussbaum
  • identify siblings and surviving branches
  • locate official pre-immigration records from 1935

Current Status

  • Official marriage record identifies Sima Krakowski, daughter of Yitzhak-Meir Krakowski and Malka.
  • Family evidence strongly supports Sima Berman (née Buxenbaum), born 24 Dec 1912 in Kovel.
  • Siblings reported by family testimony: Frida, Sonia, Sara, and Chaim — all believed to have perished in the Holocaust.
  • JHI completed a second recheck and did not find Sima's passport in their holdings.
  • JRI-Poland acknowledged the request and a substantive help-desk reply is still pending.
  • Internal Yad Vashem searching identified a real Boksenbojm branch in Kowel, but it is not yet proven to be Sima's immediate household.
  • A surviving aunt lived in Los Angeles and was in contact with Sima until around 1980. She is possibly memorialized at Mount Sinai Memorial Park, Wall Crypt 61165. Her identity is still unconfirmed.
  • William Stillman of Los Angeles sent a letter to Sima regarding the aunt. His identity and exact connection to the family are still unknown.

Surviving Aunt — Los Angeles Lead

One of the most important open leads in this investigation is Sima's only known surviving aunt.

  • Survived the Holocaust
  • Later lived in Los Angeles, California
  • Had no children
  • Remained in contact with Sima until approximately 1980
  • Believed to be memorialized at Mount Sinai Memorial Park, Los Angeles — Wall Crypt 61165
  • Her full name and identity have not yet been confirmed

We are trying to identify her and learn her story. If you knew her, knew of her, or are connected to her through any family network, please get in touch.

We are especially looking for information about her life in the United States — friends, acquaintances, or anyone who might have known her in Los Angeles.

The Last Letter — William Stillman

A letter was sent to Sima informing her of her aunt's death. The sender of that letter is believed to be William Stillman, who lived in Los Angeles.

  • Name: William Stillman
  • Location: Los Angeles, California
  • Connection: sent a letter to Sima regarding her surviving aunt
  • Exact relationship to the family is still unknown

We are trying to locate William Stillman or his descendants. He may have been a friend, neighbor, community contact, or a relative by marriage. Any information about him could help us identify the aunt and understand this branch of the family.

Key Working Problems

  1. Surname contradiction

    Official records support Krakowski, while family-memory and other evidence support Buxenbaum, Buxenboim, and Nussbaum.

  2. Missing immediate family records

    The siblings Frida, Sonia, Sara, and Chaim are still not proven directly in records.

  3. Kowel Boksenbojm cluster

    A real Boksenbojm branch exists in Kowel, but it is not yet proven as Sima’s immediate household.

  4. Surviving aunt — Los Angeles

    A surviving aunt lived in Los Angeles and was in contact with Sima until ~1980. Her identity is unconfirmed. She may be memorialized at Mount Sinai Memorial Park, Wall Crypt 61165.

  5. William Stillman — sender of the last letter

    William Stillman of Los Angeles sent a letter to Sima regarding her aunt. His connection to the family and current whereabouts or descendants are unknown.
