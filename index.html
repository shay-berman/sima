Sima Berman Birth Family Investigation

This site documents our family investigation into the first family of Sima Berman, her siblings, her father, and the relatives whose fate is still unknown.

Sima Berman is my grandmother on my father's side. My family and I are researching our family roots to understand what happened to her four siblings and her father, who most likely did not survive the Holocaust but still do not appear clearly in the records we have found. We also know that Sima did survive because she immigrated to Palestine before the Holocaust began. Another major goal is to identify the aunt who survived the Holocaust, later lived in the United States, stayed in contact with Sima for years, and may help us reconstruct the destroyed family.

Main Goal

The main goal is to understand what happened to Sima's first family.

  • find what happened to Sima's four siblings:
    • Frida
    • Sonia
    • Sara
    • Chaim
  • find what happened to her father:
    • Yerucham Meir Buxenbaum
  • identify the aunt who survived the Holocaust, later lived in Los Angeles, and stayed in contact with Sima
  • understand whether the family appears in records under:
    • Buxenbaum
    • Buxenboim
    • Krakowski
    • Nussbaum

What we are trying to clarify:

  • whether the siblings and father were murdered in the Holocaust
  • why they still do not appear clearly in the available databases
  • which surviving relatives or branches may still be traceable
  • how Sima's official and family surnames connect to the same family line

Family Summary

  • Grandmother: Sima Berman (later also appears as Sima Krakowski)
  • Born: 24 Dec 1912
  • Birthplace lead: Kovel / Kowel / Volhynia
  • Father: Yerucham Meir Buxenbaum
  • Mother: name still unknown; died when Sima was about 5.5 years old
  • Siblings: Frida, Sonia, Sara, Chaim
  • Known survivor: Sima herself, who immigrated to Palestine before the Holocaust
  • Possible surviving relative: an aunt whose name is still unknown, later in Los Angeles

Key Working Problems

  1. Destroyed first family

    We still do not have direct documentary proof for what happened to Sima's four siblings and her father, even though family memory strongly says they did not survive.

  2. Unknown aunt in Los Angeles

    We know there was a surviving aunt who was in touch with Sima in the United States, but we still do not know her name or exactly how she fits into the family tree.

  3. Surname bridge

    The records still point to more than one surname line: Buxenbaum, Buxenboim, Krakowski, and possibly Nussbaum.
