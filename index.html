Sima Berman Birth Family Investigation

This site is the web version of the repository tracking the research into Sima Berman's birth family, surname transition, Kovel origins, and archive outreach.

Sima Berman is my grandmother. My family and I are researching our family roots to understand what happened to her father and siblings during the Holocaust, and to identify whether other relatives may have survived and left additional family branches.

Open Full Research Log Open Tracker

Main Goal

Identify the birth family of:

  • Sima Berman
  • later official surname: Krakowski
  • possible earlier surname:
    • Buxenbaum
    • Buxenboim
    • Nussbaum

Current research focus:

  • reconstruct the original family from Kovel / Kowel / Volhynia
  • resolve the surname contradiction between Krakowski and Buxenbaum/Nussbaum
  • identify siblings and surviving branches
  • locate official pre-immigration records from 1935

Current Status

  • Official marriage record identifies Sima Krakowski, daughter of Yitzhak-Meir Krakowski and Malka.
  • Family evidence strongly supports Sima Berman (née Buxenbaum), born 24 Dec 1912 in Kovel.
  • JHI already replied once and is now rechecking with Kovel / Kowel as the place-of-birth anchor.
  • Internal Yad Vashem searching identified a real Boksenbojm branch in Kowel, but it is not yet proven to be Sima's immediate household.

Key Working Problems

  1. Surname contradiction

    Official records support Krakowski, while family-memory and other evidence support Buxenbaum, Buxenboim, and Nussbaum.

  2. Missing immediate family records

    The siblings Frida, Sonia, Sara, and Chaim are still not proven directly in records.

  3. Kowel Boksenbojm cluster

    A real Boksenbojm branch exists in Kowel, but it is not yet proven as Sima’s immediate household.
